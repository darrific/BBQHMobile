Our Story
1997. San Fernando, Trinidad and Tobago. “Chicken and Chips” was fast becoming the predominant ‘Fast Food’ in Trinidad and Tobago. We recognized that a lot of newly opened Fast Food Eateries did not serve foods that were Halal, ignoring the needs of the Muslim Community of Trinidad. Having worked in various eateries during the 80s, we were familiar with the various procedures involved in operating such a business enterprise. After some consideration, we decided to undertake a pioneering venture, the result of which was the creation of the Original BBQ Hut, a Fast Food Restaurant where Halal foods are served, which could be enjoyed by both Muslims and non-Muslims alike. The original location was on South Trunk Road, Bel Air, La Romaine, at a simple venue consisting of two tents and five tables. We soon began to develop and subsequently offer our now-signature line of sauces, the recipes for which are still kept secret to this day. We experienced phenomenal growth, surpassing all expectations. Due to this and other changes occurring within the Business, the venue was relocated to Cipero Road, Victoria Village, San Fernando. The streamlining created an excellent workflow, enabling orders to be filled in mere minutes, if not less. The Original BBQ Hut currently resides on 3 and 3/4 acres of prime real estate, with a staff of over 50 workers. We are now aided by our children (Cashmirr, Kamron, Abdul-Qadir, Khadijah, Celine, Aleem, Jelena, Aleemah, Alaina, and Ahmad) in the running of the business, meaning that our business is more, now than ever before, a family establishment. Our Barbecue is prized by people throughout Trinidad, and we are renowned for having one singular location.
